Gas Safety Calculatorⓘ

Estimate gas concentrations from cylinder releases or slow leaks and assess whether detection systems are required.

× What does this calculator do?

This calculator estimates the concentration of a gas released into a room and assesses whether gas detection may be required. It supports five scenarios:

  • Known Volume: You already know the volume of gas (m³) and want to calculate the resulting concentration.
  • Compressed Gas: Calculates the gas volume from cylinder pressure and water capacity, then determines the concentration.
  • Liquefied Gas: Calculates the gas volume from the weight of the contents and the specific volume, then determines the concentration.
  • Cryogenic Vessel: Calculates gas volume from the liquid volume using expansion ratios for Liquid Nitrogen or Liquid Helium.
  • Slow Leak: Estimates the steady-state concentration from a continuous gas leak in a ventilated room.

For asphyxiant gases, it calculates the resulting oxygen level - if O₂ falls below 19.5%, there is a risk of oxygen deficiency. For toxic gases, it calculates the concentration in ppm for comparison with Workplace Exposure Limits. For flammable gases, it calculates the percentage concentration for comparison with the Lower Explosive Limit (LEL).

Results represent a worst-case scenario (total release, perfect mixing) and suggest whether a detection system may be necessary.

Instructions

Important: This calculator estimates gas concentrations for a worst-case scenario. It must not be used as a replacement for a full risk assessment. You must consider all hazards related to each gas, including risks from local exposure outside of gas-air mixing.

How to use:

  1. Enter the Area/Gas Details (building, room, gas name) - these appear on the printed report.
  2. Select the calculation type that matches your situation:
    • Known Volume: You already know the gas volume in m³.
    • Compressed Gas: You have a compressed gas cylinder and need to calculate the volume from pressure and water capacity.
    • Liquefied Gas: You have a liquefied gas cylinder and need to calculate the volume from weight and specific volume.
    • Cryogenic Vessel: You have a dewar/tank of Liquid Nitrogen (LN2) or Liquid Helium (LHe).
    • Slow Leak: You want to estimate steady-state concentration from a continuous leak in a ventilated room.
  3. Enter the Room Volume (free air volume), select the Gas Hazard Type, and click Calculate Concentration.
  4. Click Generate Report for a print-friendly summary.

What you need to know:

Calculation Type Required Information
Compressed Gas Pressure (bar), water capacity of cylinder (Litres)
Liquefied Gas Weight of contents (kg), specific volume (m³/kg)*
Cryogenic Vessel Liquid volume (Litres), gas type (LN2 or LHe)
Slow Leak Leak rate (m³/hr), air changes per hour (ACH)

* Specific volume is the volume per kg of substance. See Engineering Toolbox.

Understanding results:

  • Asphyxiant gases: The calculator shows the resulting O₂ level. Below 19.5% indicates risk of oxygen deficiency; below 18% is dangerous.
  • Toxic gases: The result is shown in ppm. Compare to the Workplace Exposure Limit (WEL) in EH40.
  • Flammable gases: The result is shown as a %. Compare to the Lower Explosive Limit (LEL). Detection is required if the value exceeds 25% of the LEL.
  • Oxygen/CO₂ enrichment: Detection is required above 23.5% for O₂ or above 0.5% for CO₂.

Free air volume: This is the room volume minus space occupied by furniture and equipment. If you do not know this value, tick the checkbox to apply a 40% reduction as a conservative estimate.
