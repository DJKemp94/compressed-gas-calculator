What does this calculator do?

This calculator estimates the concentration of a gas released into a room or the remaining air composition after a hazardous release from either compressed gas cylinders or cryogenic tanks. It then assesses whether gas detection may be required. It supports five scenarios:

  • Known Volume: You already know the volume of gas (m³) and want to calculate the resulting concentration.
  • Compressed Gas: Calculates the gas volume from cylinder pressure and water capacity, then determines the concentration.
  • Liquefied Gas: Calculates the gas volume from the weight of the contents and the specific volume, then determines the concentration.
  • Cryogenic Vessel: Calculates gas volume from the liquid volume using expansion ratios for Liquid Nitrogen or Liquid Helium.
  • Slow Leak: Estimates the steady-state concentration from a continuous gas leak in a ventilated room.

Results represent a worst-case scenario (total release, perfect mixing) and suggest whether a detection system may be necessary.

Detailed Instructions & Reference Tables

△ Important: This calculator estimates gas concentrations for a worst-case scenario. It must not be used as a replacement for a full risk assessment. You must consider all hazards related to each gas, including risks from local exposure outside of gas-air mixing.

How to use:

  1. Enter the Area/Gas Details (building, room, gas name) - these appear on the printed report.
  2. Select the calculation type that matches your situation, and then provide all requested details.
  3. Click Calculate, and this will populate the fields at the bottom of the form automatically.
  4. Move down to the bottom of the form, enter the Room Volume, select the Gas Hazard Type, and click Calculate Concentration.
  5. Click Generate Report for a print-friendly summary.

What you need to know:

Compressed GasPressure (bar), water capacity of cylinder (Litres)
Liquefied GasWeight of contents (kg), specific volume (m³/kg)
Cryogenic VesselLiquid volume (Litres), gas type (LN₂ or LHe)
Slow LeakLeak rate (m³/hr), air changes per hour (ACH)

Specific volume is the volume per kg of substance. See Engineering Toolbox.

Understanding results:

  • Asphyxiant gases: Shows the resulting O₂ level. Below 19.5% = risk; below 18% = dangerous.
  • Toxic gases: Result in ppm. Compare to the WEL in EH40.
  • Flammable gases: Result as %. Detection required if >25% of the LEL for the gas.
  • Oxygen/CO₂ enrichment: Detection required above 23.5% for O₂ or above 0.5% for CO₂.

Calculate Volume - Cryogenic Vessel

Liquid nitrogen or liquid helium

Cryogenic liquids expand dramatically when released - LN₂ by ~694× and LHe by ~757× (at room temperature + atmospheric pressure).
Enter the liquid volume and select the liquid type to calculate the resulting gas volume.
△ BCGA Cryogenic Hazard Reminder: Cryogenic liquids expand ~700× when released. This can rapidly displace oxygen causing asphyxiation, create severe cold burns/frostbite on contact, and produce a dense fog reducing visibility.
